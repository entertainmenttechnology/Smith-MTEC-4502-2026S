## I. Purpose and Philosophy
- A resume is not a biography ‚Äî it‚Äôs a **strategic communication tool**.  
- The goal is to **align skills and experiences** with the expectations of your intended audience (employer, collaborator, client, or graduate program).  
- It must express both **competence and identity**, consistent with the tone of your portfolio.  
- Think of it as a **narrative interface**: the written version of your professional persona.  

---

## II. Resume Design Principles
### A. Clarity and Readability
- Prioritize **hierarchy and visual scanning** ‚Äî recruiters spend 6‚Äì10 seconds on first review.  
- Use clear headings, concise bullet points, and white space.  
- Choose a clean, legible typeface (Helvetica, Lato, Roboto, Garamond).  
- Avoid unnecessary graphics unless applying to design fields where layout is part of the evaluation.  

### B. Consistency Across Materials
- Match design elements (fonts, colors, icons) with your **portfolio‚Äôs identity**.  
- Keep tone and language consistent between resume, cover letter, and online presence.  
- Use the same **keywords and phrasing** across GitHub, LinkedIn, and portfolio sites.  

### C. Formatting and Length
- **1 page** for entry-level or early career; **2 pages max** for experienced or interdisciplinary applicants.  
- Save and submit as a **PDF** to preserve layout.  
- Include **live links** to your portfolio, LinkedIn, GitHub, or professional email.

## IV. Language and Tone
- Use **strong action verbs**: developed, produced, coordinated, designed, implemented, analyzed.  
- Quantify results where possible (‚ÄúReduced editing time by 30%‚Äù).  
- Avoid vague phrasing (‚ÄúResponsible for‚Ä¶‚Äù).  
- Emphasize collaboration and agency ‚Äî show initiative.  
- Maintain **parallel structure** across bullet points (each begins with a verb).  

---

## V. Tailoring and Customization
- Each resume should be **targeted** to a specific job or context.  
- Reorder or emphasize sections based on purpose (creative, technical, academic).  
- Extract keywords and core skills from job descriptions.  
- Keep a **master resume** with all experience; adapt a focused version for each application.

## VI. Integration with AI Tools (Ethical Use)
Use AI as a **collaborator**, not a ghostwriter.  
- Evaluate tone and clarity.  
- Identify missing keywords or redundant phrasing.  
- Compare your resume against a job description or portfolio identity.  

**Recommended Tools:** ChatGPT, Gemini, Grammarly, Teal, Jobscan, Copilot.  
**Documentation Tip:** Note AI‚Äôs role in your process: ‚ÄúUsed AI to evaluate tone and keyword alignment; final edits made manually.‚Äù

## VII. Creative and Technical Field Considerations
- **Design / Media:** May use light visual accents, icons, or subtle color; ensure legibility.  
- **Technical / Development:** Prioritize skills and project outcomes; emphasize problem-solving.  
- **Hybrid Roles:** Showcase adaptability, teamwork, and interdisciplinary understanding.

## VIII. Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Walls of text with no white space.  
- Overused buzzwords (‚Äúinnovative,‚Äù ‚Äúteam player‚Äù).  
- Listing tools without context (‚ÄúFigma‚Äù vs. ‚ÄúCreated UX prototypes in Figma for client app‚Äù).
